Wastewater treatment or management

The Water WASTE MANAGEMENT refers to the processes used to convert wastewater into an effluent that can be either returned to the water cycle with negligible environmental impact or can be reused.

  • Promote recycling to minimize water pollution and conserve resources.
  • Implement strict regulations on industrial discharges to protect water quality.
  • Educate communities on proper disposal of household chemicals to prevent contamination.

The major objective of wastewater treatment is generally to allow human and industrial effluents to be disposed of without danger to human health or unacceptable damage to the natural environment.Effective water waste management is crucial for preserving our environment and ensuring access to clean water for future generations. By promoting recycling, regulating industrial discharges, educating on proper disposal practices, and investing in advanced treatment technologies, we can safeguard water quality and sustainably manage this precious resource.

Guidelines implementation Wastewater Treatment Process

Wastewater treatment is a multi-step process designed to remove contaminants and pollutants from sewage or wastewater before it is released back into the environment or reused. The process typically involves several stages, starting with preliminary treatment where large debris and solids are removed through screening and sedimentation. Next, the wastewater undergoes primary treatment, where suspended solids are settled out further through sedimentation, and grease and oil are skimmed off the surface. Secondary treatment follows, using biological processes to break down organic matter remaining in the water. Microorganisms consume and digest organic pollutants, transforming them into harmless byproducts like carbon dioxide and water.

  • 01 Phase Separation

    Phase separation is a phenomenon where a homogeneous mixture separates into two distinct phases due to differences in the chemical properties of its components. This process occurs naturally in various systems, such as oil and water, where immiscible liquids separate into distinct layers based on their densities. Additionally, phase separation is utilized in industrial processes and laboratory settings for purification and separation purposes. Techniques such as centrifugation, filtration, and solvent extraction are employed to facilitate phase separation and isolate desired components from complex mixtures. By harnessing the principles of phase separation, scientists and engineers can efficiently purify substances, recover valuable materials, and achieve desired product specifications in diverse applications ranging from pharmaceutical manufacturing to environmental remediation.

  • 02 Filtration

    Filtration is a fundamental process used to separate solids from liquids or gases by passing them through a porous medium, such as a filter or sieve. This method relies on the size and nature of particles present in the mixture, with the filter allowing the passage of fluid while trapping and retaining solid particles. Filtration finds widespread use in various industries and everyday applications, from purifying drinking water and removing impurities from air to clarifying beverages and separating solids from chemical solutions in manufacturing processes. Different filtration techniques, including gravity filtration, vacuum filtration, and membrane filtration, are employed depending on the specific requirements of the application. Whether in industrial settings or household filtration systems, this versatile process plays a critical role in maintaining cleanliness, ensuring product quality, and protecting health and the environment by efficiently removing unwanted particles and contaminants from fluids and gases.

  • 03 Oxidation

    This process diminishes the biochemical oxygen demand of wastewater and may reduce the toxicity of some impurities. Advanced Oxidation Processes (AOPs) are a set of chemical treatment of wastewater purported to remove organic and also inorganic materials in waste water by oxidation through reaction with hydroxyl radicals. Chemical oxidation may remove some persistent organic pollutants and concentrations remaining after biochemical oxidation. Frequently Asked Questions

Waste management regulations and disposal methods.

  • What is waste management?

    Waste management is the collection, transportation and disposal of waste materials.

  • How do I practice waste management at home?

    Keep separate containers for dry and wet waste in the kitchen. Keep two bags for dry waste collection- paper and plastic, for the rest of the household waste. Keep plastic from the kitchen clean and dry and drop into the dry waste bin. Keep glass /plastic containers rinsed of food matter. Keep a paper bag for throwing sanitary waste.

  • What are the first few steps to initiate a waste management programme in your apartment complex?

    Form a group with like-minded people. Explain waste segregation to your family / neighbours in your apartment building. Get the staff in the apartment building to also understand its importance. Get separate storage drums for storing dry and wet waste. Have the dry waste picked up by the dry waste collection centre or your local scrap dealer.

  • What are the different types of waste?

    1. Wet waste- Wet waste consists of kitchen waste - including vegetable and fruit peels and pieces, tea leaves, coffee grounds, eggshells, bones and entrails, fish scales, as well as cooked food (both veg and non-veg).

    2. Dry Waste- Paper, plastics, metal, glass, rubber, thermocol, styrofoam, fabric, leather, rexine, wood – anything that can be kept for an extended period without decomposing is classified as dry waste.

    3 .Hazardous waste- Household hazardous waste or HHW include three sub-categories – E-waste; toxic substances such as paints, cleaning agents, solvents, insecticides and their containers, other chemicals; and biomedical waste.

    4. E-waste- E-waste or electronic waste consists of batteries, computer parts, wires, electrical equipment of any kind, electrical and electronic toys, remotes, watches, cell phones, bulbs, tube lights and CFLs.

    5. Biomedical waste- This includes used menstrual cloth, sanitary napkins, disposable diapers, bandages and any material that is contaminated with blood or other body fluids.

  • What are ways of storing the waste at homes?

    1. Dry waste- Store it in a bag in the utility area after cleaning and drying till it is picked up. No food residue must be left in the bottles and packets. Clean them as you would to reuse them. If clothes are totally unusable, or very damaged, they are categorized as dry waste. If clothes are soiled with body fluids, they become sanitary waste. If they are soiled with paint, or any chemicals, they are HHW (household hazardous waste).

    2. E-waste- Store them in separate container which is kept closed, away from moisture and in which nothing else is put.

  • How do I dispose my waste?

    1. Compost your wet waste at home- Home composting can easily be done in any aerated container. Get more details on composting and begin composting today!

    2 Compost your wet waste at the community level- If you live in a large apartment building, a community composting system like tank composting could be set up for all the wet waste from the residents. If not, the wet waste can be given out every day to your Municipality collection system.

    3 Biomedical waste has to be wrapped separately in a newspaper and handed over to the municipality waste collection system. Expired medicines and injections, used syringes also have to be disposed in the same manner. Paint and other hazardous waste like cosmetics, mosquito repellents, tube lights etc have to be stored separately and handed over to the Municipal collection system.
